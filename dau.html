About

After being rejected by Berlin, the film director Ilya Khrzhanovsky set up his exhibition in Paris in 3 different sites; Theatre Chatelet, Theatre de la Ville and Beaubourg. It's open for a very short period of time, but most of it is open 24/7. And you'll easily stay for at least 2-3h.
In 2009, Ilya Khrzhanovsky asked 400 people to participate in this experience; 3 years alone in a research facility in Ukraine. Cut off from the modern world, they lived in a reconstruction of life under the Societ Union; from 1938 to 1968. They were street-cleaners, global artists, philosophers, bar-workers, Nobel Prize winners, state agents, scientific, spiritual, reknowned artists and political figures and they created their own daily reality. From that experience, the film director got over 700 of rough footage and it took him 10 years to turn it all into 13 films and this exhibition. Prepare to be questionned, shook, and transformed.



Public

This is an exhibition for adults only, -18 years old are not allowed inside.



Place

The Theatre de la Ville and Theatre Chatelet are right in front of each other, and they installed big led screens with the trailer. Both of them are open 24/7. The exhibition in Centre Pompidou is only open when the museum is open, so check before going.



Tips

They don't check to time remaining you have on your pass, so if you want to do 2 places the same night, get in the second one juste an hour or two before the end of the pass and stay there as long as you want.
Don't bother taking your camera or any other electronic devices; they're not allowed. You can put your phone in little lockers, but no big lockers are available. So pack light.
